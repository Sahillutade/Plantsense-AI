STANDARD OPERATING PROCEDURE
Document No: SOP-TANK-T410-01
Title: Operation and Monitoring of Storage Tank T-410
Unit: Tank Farm
Revision: 2
Effective Date: 2021-09-01

1. PURPOSE
This procedure defines operating limits and level monitoring requirements for
storage tank T-410 (Hindustan Tank Works HTW-T500).

2. OPERATING PARAMETERS
- Maximum safe fill level: 92% of tank capacity
- High level alarm: 88%
- High-high level alarm (auto-trip of inlet valve): 90%
- Level gauge calibration check interval: quarterly

3. MONITORING REQUIREMENTS
3.1 Level shall be monitored continuously via radar-type level gauge.
3.2 Level gauge accuracy shall be verified quarterly against manual dip
    measurement, as calibration drift is a known failure mode for this
    instrument type in tanks exposed to ambient temperature variation.
3.3 Any discrepancy greater than 2% between gauge reading and manual dip
    measurement requires immediate gauge recalibration before continued reliance
    on automatic level control.

4. KNOWN FAILURE MODES
Level gauge calibration drift is the most common instrumentation issue for tanks
of this type, typically caused by ambient temperature swings affecting the radar
transmitter reference point. Drift tends to manifest gradually and may not trigger
fault alarms, making quarterly verification essential rather than relying solely
on alarm status.
