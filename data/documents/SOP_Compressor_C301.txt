STANDARD OPERATING PROCEDURE
Document No: SOP-COMP-301-01
Title: Operation and Monitoring of Reciprocating Compressor C-301
Unit: Unit 3 - Compression
Revision: 2
Effective Date: 2023-11-05

1. PURPOSE
This procedure defines the operating envelope, cooling water requirements, and trip
criteria for reciprocating compressor C-301 (Ingersoll Rand IR-RC-700).

2. OPERATING PARAMETERS
- Discharge temperature normal range: 95 - 120 C
- Discharge temperature high trip setpoint: 145 C
- Minimum cooling water flow: 40 LPM
- Cooling water inlet temperature: not to exceed 35 C

3. MONITORING REQUIREMENTS
3.1 Cooling water flow and discharge temperature are logged continuously via DCS.
3.2 A sustained drop in cooling water flow below 40 LPM for more than 5 minutes
    requires immediate operator investigation, independent of automatic trip logic.
3.3 Strainer at the cooling water inlet shall be inspected monthly for fouling, as
    restricted flow is the most common cause of high discharge temperature trips.

4. TRIP LOGIC
Compressor will automatically trip on discharge temperature exceeding 145 C. Operators
shall not reset and restart without confirming cooling water flow has been restored
and root cause identified.

5. KNOWN FAILURE MODES
Cooling water flow restriction due to strainer fouling is the predominant cause of
high discharge temperature trips for this compressor class. Seasonal cooling water
quality changes (monsoon silt loading) increase strainer fouling frequency between
June and September.
