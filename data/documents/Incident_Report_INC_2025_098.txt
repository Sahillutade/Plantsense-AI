INCIDENT REPORT
Report No: INC-2025-098
Date of Incident: 2025-09-18
Equipment Involved: C-301
Severity: Minor (no injury, production impact - 10 hours downtime)

DESCRIPTION
Compressor C-301 tripped on high discharge temperature (147 C, exceeding the 145 C
setpoint per SOP-COMP-301-01) during routine operation. Investigation identified
cooling water inlet strainer fouling, reducing flow to approximately 22 LPM against
a minimum requirement of 40 LPM.

IMMEDIATE ACTION
Compressor isolated, strainer cleaned, cooling water flow restored and verified
under WO-3155. Compressor restarted after 10 hours downtime.

CONTRIBUTING FACTORS
1. Strainer inspection interval (monthly) did not align with elevated fouling risk
   during monsoon season (June-September), as noted in SOP-COMP-301-01 Section 5.
2. No flow-based alarm existed independent of the temperature trip; flow degradation
   was not visible to operators until temperature rose.

LESSONS LEARNED
This is the second monsoon-season strainer-fouling trip on C-301 in two years.
Recommend increasing strainer inspection frequency to biweekly during June-September
and adding a low cooling water flow alarm independent of the discharge temperature
trip, to provide earlier warning before temperature limits are approached.

STATUS: Closed - recommendation pending implementation
