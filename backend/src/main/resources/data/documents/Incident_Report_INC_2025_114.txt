INCIDENT REPORT
Report No: INC-2025-114
Date of Incident: 2025-11-03
Equipment Involved: P-204
Severity: Minor (no injury, process impact only)

DESCRIPTION
During routine shift rounds, operator A. Kulkarni observed visible leakage from the
mechanical seal of pump P-204. Pump was isolated and tagged out. Inspection confirmed
seal face wear consistent with dry-running conditions, suspected to be caused by a
temporary loss of seal flush flow that was not detected by the flow alarm.

IMMEDIATE ACTION
Pump isolated, seal replaced under WO-3275. Flush flow alarm setpoint recalibrated.

CONTRIBUTING FACTORS
1. Flush flow alarm setpoint was set above the actual minimum safe flow, delaying
   detection of the flow loss.
2. No secondary indication (e.g. seal temperature) was monitored for this pump class.

LESSONS LEARNED
Pumps of this class (Kirloskar KBL-CP-450) rely on a single flow-based alarm for
seal protection. Recommend adding seal temperature monitoring as a secondary
indicator across all pumps of this model, not just P-204. This incident, combined
with the March 2026 failure (WO-3381), indicates a systemic gap in seal protection
monitoring for this equipment class rather than an isolated case.

STATUS: Closed - recommendation pending implementation across fleet
