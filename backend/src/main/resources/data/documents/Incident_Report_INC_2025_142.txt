INCIDENT REPORT
Report No: INC-2025-142
Date of Incident: 2025-08-30
Equipment Involved: T-410
Severity: Minor (no injury, no spill - caught before high-high alarm)

DESCRIPTION
During quarterly level gauge verification, manual dip measurement of tank T-410
showed actual level of 81%, while the radar level gauge displayed 76% - a 5%
discrepancy exceeding the 2% threshold defined in SOP-TANK-T410-01 Section 3.3.
Had the discrepancy gone undetected, continued filling based on the gauge reading
could have resulted in exceeding the 92% maximum safe fill level without triggering
the high level alarm (set at 88% of indicated, not actual, level).

IMMEDIATE ACTION
Inlet valve throttled, level gauge recalibrated under WO-3210. Manual dip
verification confirmed accuracy restored after recalibration.

CONTRIBUTING FACTORS
1. Ambient temperature variation during the preceding weeks (seasonal transition)
   is the suspected cause of radar transmitter reference drift, consistent with
   the known failure mode described in SOP-TANK-T410-01 Section 4.
2. Quarterly verification interval meant the drift had been present for an
   unknown duration prior to detection.

LESSONS LEARNED
This is the first recorded gauge drift incident on T-410, but the failure mode is
known to be common across radar-type level gauges installed in this tank farm.
Recommend cross-checking other tanks fitted with the same gauge model and
considering a shorter verification interval during seasonal transition periods.

STATUS: Closed - recalibration completed, fleet-wide check recommended
